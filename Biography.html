Timeline of Dr. Evelyn Shaw's Life

1927
Dr. Evelyn Shaw was born on January 19, 1927 in Jersey City, NJ.

















Everett. “Newark, New Jersey. Broad Street. Ca.” Fineartamerica
1947
She received her Bachelor of Arts in Biology at New York University.

















“New York University .” NYU Alumni.
1948
She entered the American Museum of Natural History as a Research Associate for Animal Behavior and a supervisor of a program for college students. Her research and thesis were on the black-chinned African mother breeder, she researched why the fish hasn't raised it's baby outside of it's mouth.










Skerry, Brian J. “School of Fish.” National Geographic Society.
1949
She married James Shaw, her first husband. During this time Dr. Shaw was a graduate student at NYU studying embryology. In the following years, she gave birth to 3 children named James, Russel and Susan.













“New York City in the 1950s.” FORTUNE MAGAZINE.
1952
She received her Ph.D. on Ecology and Physiology of Vertebrates at NYU. During this time, she also became an instructor in the biology department at Newark Colleges, Rutgers University. She was also an adjunct professor of biopsychology at City University.












Ecology.” Colorado State University .
1957
She officially joined the AMNH staff. She did a lot of fishing and went on a trip to the Bermuda to photograph certain aspects of underwater life and to conduct experiments.














Everett. “American Museum of Natural History.” NYC-Arts
1958
She helped with the establishment of a Division of Animal Behavior of the American Society of Zoology. Shaw was part of the Icththyology and Herpetology and Publications Committees.













“ Zoological Association of America.” ASA Affiliate Organization.
1959
She divorced James Shaw.


















Dr. Evelyn Shaw--Works with School. Daily News.
1968
The Hall of Man, which is now known as the Hall of Africans Peoples has opened and she helped curate.
















MS_170607_5823, AMNH Library
1970
She married her second husband, Frederick Wertheim who was an engineer. She then wrote many children's books including "Alligator", "Fish Out of School", "Octopus" and "A Nest of Wood Books."













Zweifel, Frances. Alligator . Harper and Row.
1984
On September 1st of 1984, the book "Female Strategies" by Evelyn Shaw and Joan Darling was published. Throughout the mid to late 70's, Shaw also co-authored multiple children's books about biology.













Female Strategies . Simon and Schuster Book.
2003
On May 19, 2003, Dr. Evelyn Shaw has passed away due to cancer at her home in San Francisco, California.
